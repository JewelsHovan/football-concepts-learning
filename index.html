Formation explorer

Shapes are starting points.

A formation tells you where players begin. Their roles and movements decide what it becomes during a match.

4–3–3: width and triangles

Three forwards stretch the pitch; a midfield three can create passing triangles. But the full-backs often have a lot to cover when play turns over.

Watch for it: Does a full-back overlap? Does a winger come inside? The listed formation may already have changed.

Make offside less mysterious.

Stats, in human language

Useful clues. Not cheat codes.

Use a number to start a conversation with the match, then watch clips or the game to understand the reason behind it.

xG

Expected goals

How likely the shots were to score. It measures chance quality better than total shots alone.

xA

Expected assists

How likely a completed pass was to become an assist, based on the shot that followed.

PPDA

Press intensity

Opponent passes allowed per defensive action. Lower often suggests more aggressive pressing.

/90

Per 90 minutes

A way to compare rates when players have played different amounts of time.
