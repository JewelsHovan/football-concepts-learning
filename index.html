Three rules for every number.

Stats are clues, not verdicts. Pair them with what happened on the pitch and they make the game more legible.

01 / CONTEXTAsk “compared with what?”

One match is noisy. A season, an opponent, and the game state tell a fuller story.

02 / QUALITYNot all actions are equal

A pass, a shot, and a tackle can look identical in a count while carrying very different value.

03 / WATCHUse your eyes too

Data points you to a pattern. Watching tells you why it happened.

Your matchday dictionary

The useful numbers, in plain English.

These four give a practical first language for modern football. Hover the highlighted words for a quick reminder.

xG

Expected goals

How good were the shots? Better for judging chances than simply counting them.

xA

Expected assists

How likely was a pass to become an assist, based on the shot that followed?

PPDA

Pressing intensity

Opponent passes allowed per defensive action. Lower often means more pressing.

+ / −

Goal difference

Goals scored minus goals conceded. A useful reality check over a run of matches.

What could you say?

City Rovers created more and better chances, but Harbour FC won. The score tells us who won; xG suggests City may have been the more threatening team.

What not to say: “City definitely deserved to win.” A match has goalkeeping, finishing, tactics, and randomness — stats describe probabilities, not justice.

“Why did City create so much but score none?” Now you can watch the clips for poor finishing, a brilliant goalkeeper, rushed shots, or a model that misses something important.
The rest of the field guide

See the patterns behind the numbers.

Stats work best when they give you better questions while you watch. Start with these three lenses.

With the ball

Possession with a purpose

Possession is time on the ball, not control by itself. Ask where a team has it and whether it creates penetration.

  • Progressive passes move the ball meaningfully toward goal.
  • Final-third entries show access to dangerous areas.
  • Shot-creating actions trace the build-up to a shot.
Without the ball

Pressing and defending

Pressing is about removing options, not just running. A low block can also be a deliberate, effective choice.

  • PPDA gives one rough picture of press intensity.
  • High turnovers are possessions won near the opponent’s goal.
  • Watch the spaces a press leaves behind.
Player context

Roles shape totals

Compare players in similar jobs. A full-back, winger and striker are asked to solve different problems.

  • Per-90 rates help when minutes differ.
  • Touches and zones explain opportunity.
  • Availability and opposition matter as much as a single total.
